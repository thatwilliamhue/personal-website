About Me  ( 01 )

Hi, my name is Will. I’m a PhD candidate and HCI researcher based in Bath, England.

My areas of interest include Personal Informatics (PI), Information Visualisation, data analytics and Computer Supported Cooperative Work (CSCW). My current projects aim to explore and improve the use of self-tracking data in the domain of chronic illnesses and healthcare. Be sure to check them out here.

I enjoy solving complex problems through information technology while leveraging the power of HCI research. Though UI design and visualisation can be vessels of artistic expression, it has never been about garish interfaces or fancy graphics. To be effective at bringing about positive change in the world through innovation, one must understand how technology would impact the work and lives of the people it touches. I enjoy taking complex problems and turning them into practical designs that user-centred and grounded in research.

In addition to my currently work, I also have experience in software engineering and web development. Having taken part in cross-functional development projects in the past, I have acquired skills which would allow me to work and build connection with people from wildly different backgrounds. I enjoy working as part of a team. Be it about the organisation of events, conferences or even fun, side projects that build rapport amongst colleagues. I will always it give my best shot. I enjoy working in a dynamic environment and I’m always ready to meet the next challenge. Ongoing personal development of skills and experience is what drives me when seeking out my next challenge.

When I’m not working, I enjoy playing music, traversing through nature and doing copious amount of outdoor activities. I also have a profound appreciation for intellectual conversations, debates and podcasts. I enjoy reading interesting and thought-provoking books across a range of topics (e.g. humanism and science) to broaden my knowledge and perspective. Check out my other interests here including a quirky portfolio which showcase the creative side of me.

Whether you are a fellow student, researcher, collaborator, employer… or none of the above. If you are interested in finding out more about me, feel free to hit me up and drop an email.
